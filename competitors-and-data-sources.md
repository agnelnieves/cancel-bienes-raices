## Competitor Analysis

### 1. Direct Competitors (National)

#### PropStream

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What they do** | All-in-one real estate investment platform. Property search, comps, skip tracing, lead lists, marketing tools (direct mail, email). Used heavily by wholesalers and flippers in the US. |
| **Pricing** | ~$99/month base. Add-ons (skip tracing, marketing) cost extra. Can easily reach $150-200/month with full usage. |
| **Strengths** | Massive US data coverage. Deep filtering (pre-foreclosure, vacant, tax lien, absentee owner). Good reputation among US investors. Integrates marketing tools directly. |
| **Weaknesses** | English only. US-mainland focused; PR data is sparse or absent. Interface is complex and intimidating for beginners. No cash deal data. Expensive for casual investors. No community or educational layer. |
| **Why we are different** | PropStream is a power tool for experienced US wholesalers. We serve Latino investors who need something accessible, bilingual, and relevant to PR. Our cash deal data gives comps PropStream simply cannot provide in PR. At $30/month vs. $99+, we are also significantly cheaper. |

#### PropWire

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What they do** | Property data and analytics platform. Focuses on providing clean comps, ownership info, and property details. Simpler than PropStream. |
| **Pricing** | Free tier with limited searches. Paid plans start around $20-30/month. |
| **Strengths** | Cleaner, simpler interface than PropStream. Lower price point. Good for investors who just need comps without the marketing suite. Growing user base. |
| **Weaknesses** | English only. Limited PR coverage. No cash deal data. Smaller dataset than PropStream. No community features. No Spanish language support. |
| **Why we are different** | PropWire is the closest in price point but lacks any PR data, Spanish support, or community integration. We match their simplicity while adding the PR market and Latino-focused experience they will never prioritize. |

#### Zillow

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **What they do** | Largest US real estate marketplace. Zestimate home values, listings, agent directory, mortgage tools. Consumer-facing, not investor-focused. |
| **Pricing** | Free for consumers. Zillow Premier Agent (advertising) costs $300-1,000+/month for agents. API access (ZTRAX) is restricted/academic. |

| **Weaknesses** | Not built for investors. Zestimates are unreliable for investment decisions, especially in non-standard markets. PR coverage is poor. No comps tool for investors. No wholesale or cash deal data. Algorithm-driven, not market-driven. English only (some limited Spanish pages but not a Spanish-first product). |
| **Why we are different** | Zillow serves homebuyers and sellers. We serve investors. Zillow's algorithms fail in PR where the market is opaque. Our human-sourced cash deal data is more accurate than any algorithm for PR investment decisions. |

#### Redfin

| Attribute | Details |

## Data Sources: Puerto Rico

### CRIM (Centro de Recaudacion de Ingresos Municipales)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Property tax assessments, property descriptions (size, type, location), catastro numbers, assessed valuations, tax payment status, owner of record. |
| **Access model** | Public records. Accessible via their website (manual lookup by catastro number). No official API. Bulk data may be obtainable through formal FOIA-equivalent requests (Ley de Transparencia). |
| **Cost** | Free for manual lookups. Bulk data requests may involve administrative fees. |
| **Reliability** | Official government data. However, assessed values are often significantly below market value and updated infrequently. Property descriptions can be outdated. |
| **How it fits our product** | Foundation layer for PR property data. Every property has a catastro number and CRIM record. We use this as our base dataset, then enrich it with market data. Assessed values serve as a floor/reference point but should never be presented as market value. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Start with manual data collection for key municipalities (San Juan, Bayamon, Carolina, Ponce, Caguas). Explore scraping (check legal compliance). File formal data requests. Long term: advocate for or build a CRIM data partnership. |

### Registro de la Propiedad (Property Registry)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Deed records, ownership transfers, liens, encumbrances, property legal descriptions, sale prices (when recorded), mortgage information. |
| **Access model** | Public records. In-person searches at registry offices (there are multiple registries across PR by geographic region). Some records may be available through their modernization efforts online, but coverage varies. |
| **Cost** | Small fees per search/document ($5-25 per record). In-person searches may require a title searcher or attorney. |
| **Reliability** | Official and legally binding. However, not all transactions include sale prices (especially cash deals, which may record as nominal amounts). Registration can lag behind actual transactions. |
| **How it fits our product** | Critical for ownership verification and transfer history. Sale prices from recorded deeds provide official comps data. Combined with our cash deal network data, this gives us the most complete picture available. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Partner with title search companies or attorneys who already access the registry regularly. Explore digital records where available. Build relationships with registry staff for bulk access. |

### PR MLS Systems

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Active listings, sold data (agent-reported), property details, listing photos, days on market, price changes, agent information. |
| **Access model** | MLS access in PR is controlled by local REALTOR associations. Primary systems include PRMLS. IDX/RETS feeds require a brokerage relationship or data licensing agreement. |
| **Cost** | MLS membership fees ($200-500/year through a brokerage). IDX/RETS data feed licensing varies ($50-500/month). Some data may be accessible through partnerships with brokerages. |
| **Reliability** | Good for listed properties. However, MLS participation in PR is lower than US mainland (many transactions happen outside MLS). Cash deals and FSBO properties are typically absent. |
| **How it fits our product** | Important data source for listed properties and reported sold prices. The gap between MLS data and actual market activity (cash deals, off-MLS transactions) is exactly where our proprietary data fills in. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Establish a brokerage partnership or IDX agreement with PRMLS. Christopher's realtor network is the key here. A friendly brokerage can provide an IDX feed that gives us listed and sold data. |

### Court Records (Foreclosures)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Foreclosure filings (lis pendens), auction notices, court-ordered sales, bankruptcy filings involving real property, judgments, tax lien proceedings. |
| **Access model** | PR court system (Rama Judicial). Some records searchable online through the Oficina de Administracion de Tribunales (OAT) portal. Many records still require in-person courthouse visits. |
| **Cost** | Online searches are generally free. Certified copies have fees ($1-5 per page). |
| **Reliability** | Official court records. Reliable for identifying distressed properties. However, data is not structured for easy aggregation. Requires parsing legal documents. |
| **How it fits our product** | Pre-foreclosure and foreclosure data is highly valuable for investors looking for distressed deals in PR. We can build alerts and lists of properties entering foreclosure. This is data that no current PR platform aggregates. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Build a scraping system for the OAT online portal. Hire a paralegal or researcher to do periodic courthouse searches in key jurisdictions. Monitor legal notice publications (La Gaceta, local newspapers). |

### Municipal Permit Offices (OGPE / Municipios)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Building permits, renovation permits, zoning information, certificates of use, property compliance status. |
| **Access model** | Varies by municipality. OGPE (Oficina de Gerencia de Permisos) handles some centralized permitting. Individual municipalities also issue permits. Some online portals exist. |
| **Cost** | Records requests are generally free or low cost. |
| **Reliability** | Official government data but fragmented across 78 municipalities. Coverage and digitization varies widely. |
| **How it fits our product** | Permit data helps investors understand renovation activity (a leading indicator of gentrification/appreciation) and verify that properties are up to code. Useful for ARV estimates and investment risk assessment. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Start with major municipalities (San Juan, Bayamon, Carolina). Focus on OGPE's centralized system first. Low priority initially; add as an enrichment layer later. |

### Cash Deal Network (Christopher's Realtor Contacts)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Actual sale prices of cash transactions, property details, buyer/seller info (with consent), deal timelines, property condition at time of sale. |
| **Access model** | Proprietary. Built through personal relationships with realtors who close cash deals. Incentive-based: realtors share data in exchange for compensation or platform benefits. |
| **Cost** | Incentive payments to participating realtors ($25-100 per verified deal submission, or percentage-based). Platform features/leads as non-cash incentive. |
| **Reliability** | Highly reliable if verified. These are actual transaction prices from parties involved in the deal. Must implement verification process (cross-reference with deed recordings when available). |
| **How it fits our product** | THIS IS OUR PRIMARY COMPETITIVE MOAT. Cash deals in PR are frequently unreported or underreported. Having actual cash transaction prices gives us comps data that no other platform can provide. This is the single most valuable data source we have. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Build a simple submission system (mobile-friendly form or app). Start with Christopher's immediate network (target 10-20 realtors initially). Offer launch incentives. Create a "verified contributor" badge/status. Scale by making the platform valuable enough that realtors want to be part of it. Long term: this becomes a two-sided network effect. |

### Banks and Credit Unions with REO Properties

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | REO (Real Estate Owned) property listings, asking prices, property condition reports, disposition timelines. Banks with significant PR REO portfolios include: Banco Popular, FirstBankPR, Oriental Bank, and HUD (for FHA-insured properties). |
| **Access model** | Some banks list REO properties on their websites or through services like Hubzu. Direct relationships with asset managers can provide earlier access. HUD homes are listed on HUDHomeStore.com. |
| **Cost** | Free to access public listings. Direct relationships may require becoming an approved buyer/broker. |
| **Reliability** | Good. Bank-listed prices are real asking prices. Sold data may be harder to obtain without relationships. |
| **How it fits our product** | REO properties are a significant source of investment opportunities in PR. Tracking bank dispositions gives us data on distressed property pricing and helps our users find deals. |
| **Data acquisition strategy** | Scrape public REO listing pages from major PR banks. Build relationships with bank asset managers. Monitor HUDHomeStore.com for PR properties. Christopher's network likely includes contacts at local banks. |

## Data Sources: United States

### RentCast API

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | Rental estimates, rent comparables, rental market trends, long-term rental value estimates by property type and location. |
| **Access model** | REST API with JSON responses. Self-serve signup. Well-documented. |
| **Cost** | Free tier: 50 API calls/month. Paid plans: ~$50/month (500 calls), ~$150/month (2,000 calls), ~$500/month (10,000 calls). Custom enterprise pricing available. |
| **Reliability** | Good for rental estimates in major US markets. Uses a combination of MLS, listing, and census data. Less reliable in rural or thin markets. |
| **How it fits our product** | Essential for rental investors. Allows us to show estimated rental income and cash flow analysis alongside comps. Key for buy-and-hold investors in our community. Start here for US rental data since the API is accessible and affordable. |
| **Integration notes** | Simple REST API. Can be integrated quickly. Rate limits apply per plan. Good developer documentation. |

### Zillow API / ZTRAX

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Data provided** | **Zillow API (Bridge Interactive):** Listings, Zestimates, property details, market trends. **ZTRAX (Zillow Transaction and Assessment Dataset):** Comprehensive historical transaction and assessment data for 2,750+ counties. |
| **Access model** | Zillow API: Requires application and approval. Restricted usage policies (must display Zillow branding, link back to Zillow). ZTRAX: Academic/research access only through application to Zillow. |
| **Cost** | Zillow API: Free tier available, paid plans for higher volume. ZTRAX: Free for approved academic research. Not available for direct commercial use. |

### Data Quality Principles

1. **Always label data provenance.** Users should know if a comp comes from MLS, our cash deal network, public records, or an algorithm.
2. **Cash deal data is our moat.** Invest disproportionately in growing and verifying this dataset.
3. **PR data first, US data second.** We win by being the best in PR, then expand.
4. **Verify before trusting.** Cross-reference data sources where possible (e.g., CRIM assessment vs. MLS sold price vs. cash deal report).
5. **Never show stale data as current.** Always show data timestamps and freshness indicators.
